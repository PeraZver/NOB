# 2nd Dalmatian Proletarian Brigade

## Formation
 - **Date of formation**: October 3, 1942
 - **Place of formation**: Uništa, near Bosansko Grahovo
 - **Constituent battalions**: 
 	- battalion "Branko Vladušić"
 	- battalion "Jerko Ivančić"
 	- battalion "Nikola Vrančić"
 	- Littoral battalion "Branko Vladušić"

 - **Strength at the time**: 836 fighters						 
 - **Commander**: Ljubo Vučković
 - **Commisar**: Ante Jurlin Marko


# Operations
From its establishment until November 1, 1942, the brigade was subordinated to the Headquarters of the Fourth Operational Zone of Croatia. It was part of the Second Proletarian Division from November 1, 1942 to February 23, 1944, and then part of the Coastal Operational Group of the Second Strike Corps until January 5, 1945, and the Ninth Division of the Eighth Dalmatian Corps until the end of the war.


The 2nd Dalmatian Brigade participated in the 2nd Proletarian Division's attacks on Bosansko Grahovo in October 1942, Livno and Tomislavgrad in December 1942, and Imotski in February 1943.


### Battles of Neretva and Sutjeska
In the offensive actions of the Main Operational Group, under the command of the Supreme Staff, in the 4th enemy offensive, the Brigade participated in the liberation of Posušje and Imotski, on February 9 and 10, and it fought particularly hard battles with Chetnik forces and parts of the 781st German Division in the Neretva Valley, at the end of February and the beginning of March 1943, and then in the Battle of the Neretva. Its successful crossing of the Neretva river on February 6/7 is remembered. 
In March 1943, during the crushing of the Chetniks on the left bank, which enabled the Main Operational Group of the Supreme Staff and the Central Hospital of the Supreme Staff to cross the river, where there were about 4,000 wounded. 
In the second half of March, the brigade participated in the defeat of the Chetnik forces near Glavatičevo and Kalinovik, and in very difficult conditions they crossed the river Drina, destroyed the Chetnik bunkers and destroyed theirs and Italian forces, and formed a bridgehead for the other units of the 2nd Proletarian Division to cross the river. She was praised by the decree of the NOVJ Supreme Headquarters for forcing the Drina on April 9. In the second half of May, the brigade fought very hard battles with units of the 1st German Mountain Division on Sinjajevina, near Mojkovac, on the left bank of the Tara.
In the Battle of Sutjeska, it played a key role at a critical moment with her fanatical defense of her position on Bare in continuous battles with German forces, where she lost around 300 fighters and elders. Of all the 16 NOVJ brigades that participated in the battle on Sutjeska, the 2nd Dalmatian Brigade had the biggest losses - 833 fighters of this brigade were killed during the one-month operation.


### Fighting in Bosnia, Sandžak and Serbia
As part of the Main Operational Group, it participated in offensive operations through eastern Bosnia and led successful battles in Romania. During the liberation of Kladnje, near Tuzla, Banovići and Ozren, and in mid-September 1943, it stood out in the liberation of Trnovo and Dobro Polje. During October, it again operated on the terrain of Montenegro, where already on October 4 it assisted the 4th Montenegrin Brigade in defeating the Italian-Chetnik forces that attacked Kolašin, and from October 18 to 20, it repelled the attempt by parts of the 297th German Division to break through from Peć towards Berane and Andrijevica. After successful battles on the field of Pljevlja, the 2nd Dalmatian brigade, along with the 2nd proletarian and 1st Šumadija brigades, it was transferred to Serbia, on December 21 where it fought constant battles against Bulgarian and Chetnik forces on Zlatibor, around Nova Varoš, Ivanjica and Arilje until January 23, 1944. After those battles, the brigade returned to Kolašin via Sandžak, where on February 23, it joined the Primorska operational group.


### Fighting in Montenegro and Croatia
Together with parts of the Zeta and Lovcen NOP detachments, it broke Chetniks in the field of Ubli, Čevo, Bata on March  3/4. 1944. In the middle of March, the 3rd and parts of the 2nd battalion of the 1st Dalmatian Brigade, which after the breakthrough on Sutjeska fought as part of the 10th Herzegovinian Brigade, entered its composition. At the beginning of April, together with the 6th Montenegrin Brigade, it defeated the German and Chetnik forces near Stubica, on the Danilovgrad-Nikšić road, and from the beginning of May, it fought against the 369th German Division and the Chetniks in the area of Grab, Mrcine, Vrbanja and around Bileća. 
From August 7 to 10, it fought hard battles with the German 181st Division, and with about 1,400 Chetniks on the field of Vilus. It liberated Dubrovnik with units of the 29th Herzegovinian Division on October 18, 1944. 
In the Mostar operation, it liberated Široki Brijeg and Mostar. Then, in the operations of the 4th Army, he participated in the liberation of Karlobag, Rab, Cres, Lošinj and Istria. The combat journey ended with participation in the liberation of Trieste and Rijeka. The Brigade ended its journey with the battle near the village of Spajana on May 6, 1945.

5,548 fighters fought in the brigade, of which 209 were foreign nationals (188 Italians). Of these, 1,829 fighters died. Its 23 fighters were declared national heroes of Yugoslavia.
