# 1st Dalmatian Proletarian Brigade


## Formation
 - **Date of formation**: September 6, 1942
 - **Place of formation**: Dobro, near Livno
 - **Constituent battalions**: 
 	- battalion "Ante Jonić" 
 	- battalion "Bude Borjan"
 	- battalion "Josip Jurčević"
 	- battalion "Tadija Anušić"

 - **Strength at the time**: 1,217 fighters						 
 - **Commander**: Pero Ćetković
 - **Commisar**: Ante Kronja

When the brigade was formed, it had four battalions, named after prominent partisan fighters and revolutionaries from Dalmatia — Ante Jonić, Buda Borjan, Josip Jurčević and Tadija Anušić. Of the total of 1,217 fighters it had on the day of its formation, 83 were women. There were 1,184 fighters from Dalmatia, namely — from Split 204, Šibenik 182, Knin 143, Sinj 138, Makarska 129, Trogir 119, Vrgorac 62, Omiš 32, Korčula 22, Ploče 60, Zadar 15, Benkovac 4, Biograd 9, Drniš 16, Brač 3, Hvar 15, Imotski 4, Vis 20, Metković 7, and 33 from outside Dalmatia.

## Operations
From its formation until November 11, 1942, it was under the command of the Headquarters of the 4th Operational Zone of Croatia, and then became part of the 3rd Strike Division. From September 1943 to January 1944, it was part of the 9th Dalmatian Division, and then part of the 26th Dalmatian Division, where it remained until the end of the war. 

### Battle on Neretva
While under the command of the Headquarters of the 4th Operational Zone of Croatia, during September and October 1942, it participated in the battles near Sinj, around Duvno and in the Livno-Sinj area. From joining the 3rd Strike Division in November 1942 until the battle on the Neretva, it participated in the battles for the liberation of Jajce at the end of November, and then in the battles for its defense. Brigade commander **Božo Bilić Marjan** was killed in these battles. After that, it was engaged in battles in the Vrbas valley and central Bosnia. During the battle on the Neretva, it distinguished itself in — the capture of Prozor, February 16 and 17; in the battles in the Rama valley; in the counterattack of the Main Operational Group of the Supreme Staff, from March 3 to 5 near Gornji Vakuf and again in the battles in the Neretva valley. In the offensive of the units of the Main Operational Group from the Neretva to the east, the 1st Dalmatian Brigade was engaged in battles on Prenja, near Fazlagić Kula, Kobilja Glava and other places in Herzegovina.

### Battle on Sutjeska and central Bosnia
During the battle on Sutjeska, from mid-May to mid-June 1943, the First Dalmatian Brigade performed several difficult tasks — first of all, it led heavy battles near Šavnik, on the Komarnica, Piva and Tara rivers; and then she made an attempt for part of the Main Operational Group to break into Sandžak. After that, it broke out on Vučevo, and on June 11, 1943, its three battalions and the First Battalion of the 5th Proletarian Montenegrin Brigade formed a special group that tried to form a bridgehead on the left bank of the Sutjeska, in the area of Tjentište. On the same day, these forces defeated the enemy and began to advance towards Zelengora. Under the pressure of many times stronger enemy forces, a bridgehead was not created, so until the outbreak of parts of the 5th Proletarian Montenegrin Brigade, it fought in the area of Vrbničke kolibe, after which both, with the main forces, moved towards Jahorina and Romania. Its 3rd Battalion was cut off at Jahorina, leaving two battalions (its 2nd Battalion was destroyed at Sutjeska). During July and the first half of August 1943, it was engaged in battles in eastern and central Bosnia. The Third Battalion and part of the Second Battalion made their way into Herzegovina, where they joined the 10th Herzegovinian Brigade. 

### Reorganization and Fighting in Dalmatia 
By decision of the Supreme Headquarters of NOV and POJ, the brigade was sent to Dalmatia, and arrived in the Vrlika region on August 20 where three Dalmatian battalions entered its composition. The 1st Dalmatian Brigade joined the 9th Dalmatian Division when it was formed on 8 September 1943 in Imotski. From 10 to 26 September, it participated in heavy fighting preventing the 7th SS Division "Prince Eugen" of Knin from penetrating into Split, as well as in the defense of Split.

During October 1943, it closed the routes from Imotski and Posušje to Livno and Duvno and fought hard against the 5th Ustasha Group. At the end of the same month, via the port of Podgora, it was transferred to Pelješac, where it fought against the 7th SS Division for ten days together with the 13th Dalmatian Brigade. It fought for a week near Zagvozd and Grabovac, and on 16/17 November it was transferred to Hvar, where it organized the defense with the 11th Dalmatian brigade and held it until December 23. In order to help other forces, it was transferred to the island of Korčula on the 23rd/24th. December, but it was forced to withdraw.


### Regrouping at Vis and liberation of Dal
In January 1944, it 9th the Ninth Dalmatian Division and joined the 26th Dalmatian Division, after which it was tasked with organizing the defense of the island of Vis with other units of the division. From here, it carried out dozens of operations on the islands and the Pelješac peninsula:
 - on January 24, it carried out a landing on Hvar in an unsuccesfull attempt to retake the island
 - its 3rd Battalion assisted the 11th Dalmatian Brigade in landing on the island of Mljet on April 20/21, and a night later in the landing on the island of Korčula 
 - On May 10, it carried out a successful landing on the island of Šolta
 - with other forces of the 26th division and British commandos, on June 1, 1944, it attempted landing on the island of Brač and retreated after four days of fighting
 - On July 21, part of the force landed on Pelješac
 - on September 8, the brigade recaptured island Hvar, and Brač, where it defeated parts of the 738th regiment of the German 118th division.

The 1st brigade participated in several battles for the final liberation of Dalmatia — on September 23 for the liberation of Hvar; in mid-October, she carried out a landing on the coast south of the Neretva, near Vukovo Klanac, where she participated with the 11th Dalmatian Brigade in the destruction of the battle group of the 369th Legionary Division; then it led the battles for the liberation of Šibenik (October 3), and in the Knin operation in November/December 1944. In April and May 1945 it fought several heavy battles the Lika-Littoral and Trieste operations. Its war journey ended at Soča on May 8, 1945.

During the National Liberation War, about 5,500 soldiers passed through the First Dalmatian Brigade, of which 2,229 soldiers died.
