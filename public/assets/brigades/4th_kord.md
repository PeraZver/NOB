# 4th Kordun Assault Brigade

## Formation
 - **Date of formation**: August 20, 1942
 - **Place of formation**: Gornji Budački, Kordun
 - **Constituent battalions**: 
   - Assault battalion of the First Kordun NOP Detachment
   - Assault battalion of the Second Kordun NOP Detachment
   - Youth battalion "Joža Vlahović"
 - **Strength at the time**: Approximately 900 fighters
 - **Commander**: Information not available
 - **Commissar**: Information not available

## Operations

The 4th Kordun Assault Brigade was formed on August 20, 1942, in Gornji Budački, Kordun, from the assault battalions of the First and Second Kordun NOP detachments and the youth battalion "Joža Vlahović." Initially, the brigade comprised around 900 fighters. By December 1942, a fourth battalion was added, increasing the brigade's strength to approximately 1,300 fighters.

Throughout its existence, the 4th Kordun Assault Brigade was part of the 8th Division and participated in various operations alongside the division and the 4th Corps. The brigade was actively involved in the National Liberation War, contributing significantly to the efforts against Axis forces in Yugoslavia.

The brigade's notable achievements include being awarded several honors for its role in the liberation efforts. These honors include the Order of the People's Liberation, the Order of the Partisan Star, and the Order of Brotherhood and Unity. These decorations highlight the brigade's commitment and effectiveness in combat during the war.

The 4th Kordun Assault Brigade's operations were characterized by their strategic importance and the bravery of its fighters, contributing to the broader success of the Yugoslav Partisan movement in World War II.
