# Battle of Vukov Klanac (15–23 October 1944)

The battle of Vukov Klanac was fought during the Allied autumn offensive in southern Dalmatia as Axis forces retreated from Dubrovnik and Ston toward Metković and beyond. 

## Context and Location

- Took place between Ston and Metković, from the Neretva River and western Pelješac to Popovo Polje in the north.  
- Part of broader operations by the Yugoslav 8th Corps targeting Axis withdrawal routes.

## Course of the Battle

- 15 October: Fighting begins in the Vukov Klanac area.  
- Partisan forces first focused on liberating Ston, a heavily fortified town with bunkers, mines, and wire defenses.  
- By 18 October, partisans broke German and Italian fascist and fully liberated Ston.  
- 19–23 October: Intense combat occurred around Oštrikovac and Vukov Klanac, with German attempts to break through partisan positions ultimately failing.  
- 23 October: German Kampfgruppe “Becker” was forced to *surrender* after repeated partisan attacks and blocked supply routes.

## Outcome and Losses

- Partisan victory, marking one of the first times during the liberation of Dalmatia that two NOVJ brigades defeated a German division in direct combat.  
- Axis casualties and captures numbered around 2,000, with significant equipment losses (including tanks, artillery, vehicles).  
- Partisan losses about 56 killed and ~260 wounded.
