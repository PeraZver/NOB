# 28th Slavonian Division

## Formation
 - **Date of formation**: May 17, 1943
 - **Place of formation**: near villages Ljutoc and Vucjak on Papuk mountain
 - **Constituent brigades**: 
 	- 17th Slavonian Brigade (from April 1944)
 	- 21st Slavonian brigade
 	- 25th Brod Brigade (joined in February 1944)
 	- 3rd Artillery Brigade (from April 1945)
 - **Strength at the time**: 1,670 fighters 							 
 - **Commander**: Vicko Antić Pepe
 - **Commisar**: Vlado Janić Capo


## Operations
In the two years of the war, the 28th division fought in Slavonia, northwestern Croatia, Turopolje (near Velika Gorica), central and eastern Bosnia, participated in the battle for the liberation of Belgrade, then in the battles near Bijeljina and Brčko, and finally in the final operations for the liberation of Yugoslavia. As part of the 2nd Army, the 28th Division operated in the operational direction of Doboj, Derventa, Gradiška, Bosanska and Hrvatska Kostajnica, Petrinja, and ended its war journey in the battles for the liberation of Zagreb, entering Zagreb via the Sava Bridge on May 8, 1945. During the battles in Slavonia, it was part of the 6th Corps of the NOVJ, and during the first and second penetration into northwestern Croatia under the command of the 2nd Operational Zone of Croatia, i.e. the 10th Corps of the NOVJ. After crossing the Sava in August 1944, it was subordinated to the headquarters of the 3rd Shock Corps, and then during the Belgrade operation to the headquarters of the 12th Corps. After that, it fought in northeastern Bosnia as part of the Southern Operational Group NOVJ, and then from January 1, 1945, as part of the 2nd Army of JA.
