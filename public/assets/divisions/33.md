# 33rd Croatian Division

## Formation
 - **Date of formation**: January 19, 1944
 - **Place of formation**: Koprivnica
 - **Constituent brigades**:
 
    - 1st Moslavina Brigade
    - 2nd Moslavina Brigade
    - 3rd Moslavina Brigade "Nikola Demonja" (from September 1944) 
 - **Strength at the time**: 1,665 fighters                
 - **Commander**: Josip Antolović – Hrvat
 - **Commisar**:  - 


## Early Operations (January–March 1944)
The division conducted constant attacks against Wehrmacht, Ustaša and Home Guard forces, focusing especially on sabotaging railways and communications on the Dugo Selo – Banova Jaruga, Dugo Selo – Križevci, and Križevci – Bjelovar lines.

Key actions included:
- Capture of Sveti Ivan Žabno (28 January).  
- Repelling Ustaša attacks near Grabrovec (30 January) and Rovišće (31 January).  
- Destruction of ~15 km of track and infrastructure between Žabljak and Sv. Ivan Žabno (31 January–1 February).  
- Battles near Veliki Zdenci, Dabac, Lipovac, and Srpska Kapela (February).  
- Ambush of a German column near Srpska Kapela (26 February).  
- Engagement with Home Guard forces between Hercegovac and Ladislav (14 March).  
- Destruction of a transport train near Rovišće, halting traffic for 50 hours (4 March).  
- Near-encirclement at Oborovo (29 March), after which the 2nd Brigade broke out with heavy losses.

## Spring–Summer 1944
- The 1st Brigade operated in Hrvatsko Zagorje and Kalnik; the 2nd Brigade in Moslavina and western Bilogora.  
- Unsuccessful attacks on Sv. Ivan Žabno (1 May) and Rovišće (7 May).  
- Participation in attacks on Kloštar Ivanić (15 May) and Nart (25 May).  
- Series of railway destructions in June near Ivanić-Grad, Prečec, Vrbovec, and Gradec.  
- Heavy fighting in mid-June near Ladislav, Pašijan, Zdenčac, and Mlinska.  
- Withdrawal toward Bilogora after clashes with the German 1st Cossack Cavalry Division and 1st Home Guard Mountain Regiment (24 June).  
- From 27 June to 2 July, the division—together with the 32nd and 28th Divisions—repelled Ustaša–Home Guard attacks on Kalnik and Podravina.

## Second Half of 1944
- July–August: repeated demolition of railways (Križevci–Dugo Selo, Križevci–Bjelovar); fighting near Kloštar Ivanić and Ivanska; liberation of Dubrovčak and Mahovo (10 August).  
- 25 September: formation of the “Miloš Obilić” (later “Nikola Demonja”) Brigade as the division’s Third Brigade.  
- Autumn operations in Podravina and Bjelovar district:  
  - Liberation of Kloštar (5 October) and Novigrad Podravski (11/12 October).  
  - Repelling Ustaša attacks and striking German garrisons (e.g., Šumećani on 30/31 October).  
  - Liberation of Hlebine (3 November), Brckovljani, Božjakovina, Gradec (7/8 November), Šumećani (13/14 November), Peteranec (16 November), Marča (20 November), Donji Gudovac and Veliko Korenovo (2 December), Batina and Husain (7/8 December).  
- By late December, the division had 4,034 fighters with substantial weaponry (1,693 rifles, 247 automatics, 180 machine guns, 15 mortars, 7 AT guns, 14 “John Bull” launchers).

## 1945 Operations
- January: defense of the Virovitica bridgehead (Sedlarica, Brzaja, Vukosavljevica, Mali Grđevac).  
- February: fighting around Daruvar and Garešnica (liberated on 27 February).  
- 8–17 March: operations in Moslavina and Bjelovar area, repelling German and Ustaša–Home Guard attacks on free territory.  
- Liberation of Tomaš (18 March); repulsing attacks near Veliki Grđevac, Pavlovac, Velika Jasenovac (22 March).  
- Late March–April: victories near Palancani (28 March), Jakšić (17 April), and Međurić (19/20 April).  

The 33rd Division took part in the final liberation of Moslavina and Bilogora.
