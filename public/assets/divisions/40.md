# 40th Slavonian Division

## Formation
 - **Date of formation**: July 15, 1944
 - **Place of formation**: vicinity of Daruvar
 - **Constituent brigades**:
  
 	- 16th Youth Brigade "Joža Vlahović"
 	- 18th Slavonian Brigade
 	- Virovitica Brigade (joined in August 1944)
 - **Strength at the time**: 4,700 fighters 							 
 - **Commander**: Veljko Kovačević (until December 1944), Savo Miljanović
 - **Commisar**: Ivan Vondraček



## Operations
At the end of July 1944, it operated in the Daruvar Valley. With the 12th and 28th Divisions, it participated in the liberation of Badljevina on August 2, Končanica on August 9, and Grubišno Polje on August 18.

The Virovitica Brigade joined it on August 28. The division then had about 4,700 fighters. After moving to the Slavonian Požega region, it fought in that area until mid-September, and then again in the Daruvar Valley, where it liberated Pakrac and Lipik on September 14, and Daruvar on September 16. In the battles of September 25 and 26, it liberated Podravska Slatina, and in early October it cooperated with units of the 10th Corps and the 12th Division in the liberation of Virovitica and Pitomača.

It participated in the battles around Koprivnica from October 13/14 to 16, after which it was sent to the Banova Jaruga–Batrina railway, where it operated until early November, and then transferred to the Našice–Osijek communication.

In the battles from November 18 to 28, it failed to capture Našice, but on December 2, it liberated Pleternica. On that day, its units also invaded Slavonska Požega, occupied a spirits factory, a hospital and several fortified buildings, but quickly retreated. At the end of December 1944, the division had grown to 6,278 fighters.

Until the beginning of January 1945, it carried out diversions on the Pleternica-Našice-Osijek road. From January to the beginning of April, together with units of the Tenth Corps, it operated in the area of Koprivnica and Bjelovar, cooperating with units of the Third Army. On January 31, the division gained the title "Strike" Division.

In the final operations, breaking through Papuk and the Požega valley to meet the units of the First and Third Army of the JA, in the first half of April, after several days of heavy fighting against the German forces near Našice and Podgorac, the division together with the Twelfth Division prevented the Germans from organizing defense in the area of those places. In the second half of April, it participated in the liberation of Našice, Kutjevo and Vetovo, and then on April 21, it joined the Third Army of the Republic of Serbia. In cooperation with the 36th Vojvodina Division, it liberated Grubišno Polje on May 1, and then took part in the pursuit of the enemy in the direction of Križevci–Hum–Lepoglava–Ptujska gora.
