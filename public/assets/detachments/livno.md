# Livno partisan detachment

## Formation
The Livno partisan detachment was formed on October 14, 1941 on Mount Cincaru, in the size of the company.

## Operation
It carried out minor actions on the Kupres-Šuica-Livno road, and on June 6, 1942, it was renamed to the "Vojin Zirojević" battalion in the 4th Operational Zone of Croatia. 
In August 1942, after the arrival of a group of proletarian and strike brigades, the battalion was significantly strengthened, and in cooperation with the units of the Group, participated in the liberation of Livno and Duvno. With the Duvno battalion, it was part of the 3rd NOP detachment of the 4th operational zone from September 3rd, and in November 1942, both battalions were merged into one - Livno Battalion, which as the 5th battalion became part of the 10th Herzegovinian brigade. 

## Disbandment and  formation of Glamoč-Livno Detachment
The newly formed Livno Battalion, after leaving Livno in the 4th eemy offensive (Operation "Weiß"), was disbanded in February 1943 to fill the units of the 3rd Division. 
In June 1943, partisan groups from the lower Livno field gathered in a company joined the Glamoč detachment, which then changed its name to the Glamočko-Livanjski NOP Detachment. 

## Livno-Duvno Detachment in 20th division
In September 1943, the Livno detachment is formed again, and in the first half of October subordinated to the newly formed 20th division. Since February 1944, the detachment has been part of the 5th Corps of the NOVJ, and on April 11, 1944, it merged with the Duvno NOP detachment (formed in the meantime) into the Livanjsko-Duvanjski NOP detachment, with a strength of two battalions. 

Carrying out operations against Ustasha militia and Chetniks in Livanjsko and Duvanjsko Polje, the Detachment constantly grew in numbers, so that in July, when it joined the 10th Division, it had three battalions. Together with parts of the 1st and 6th proletarian and 9th and 10th strike divisions, it participated in the battles with parts of the German 7th SS "Prince Eugen", 118th and 369th divisions, during the capture and liberation of Livno, Duvno, Šuica. 
On October 10, 1944, the Glamoč Detachment entered its composition as the 4th battalion, and by the end of the year, two more battalions were formed, when the detachment had over 1,000 fighters and controlled the free territory between the mountains of Dinara, Šator, Vitorog and Ljubuša. The detachment was disbanded in February 1945, as two battalions each were given to fill the 1st brigade of the 3rd KNOJ division and the 17th Krajina (Ram) Strike Brigade of the 10th division; other brigades of the same division were filled with the remaining fighters.
